Systematic review of 26 systematic reviews on digital interventions for ADHD (34,000+ participants). Positive effects on inattention and executive function. Published in peer-reviewed venue.

**Study #2 — Gamified Digital Mental Health Interventions (Meta-Analysis, 2024)**
Meta-analysis of 27 randomized controlled trials examining gamified digital mental health interventions for ADHD and depression. Effect sizes: ADHD g ≈ 0.28; Depression g ≈ 0.28. Computer-delivered interventions produced larger effects than other modalities.

**Study #3 — Digital Psychoeducation for Adult ADHD (2025)**
Real-world audit and pilot RCTs of smartphone/computer-based ADHD apps. Psychoeducation apps delivering CBT techniques produced reductions in ADHD symptoms and functional impairment. App-based group completed more homework and showed greater symptom reduction vs. pen-and-paper controls.

**Study #4 — Multiverse Meta-Analysis of Digital Interventions for Depression (2024)**
Synthesis of 125 randomized controlled trials (32,733 participants) on digital interventions for depression. Average effect size Hedges' g = 0.43 (medium-to-large clinical effect). Effects consistent even across alternative analytic assumptions.

**Study #5 — Computer-Assisted Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CCBT)**
Multiple trials and APA clinical summaries showing CCBT equivalent to face-to-face CBT when clinician-supported. Addresses barriers of cost, location, and time. Internet-based CBT programs equivalent to face-to-face therapy for depression.

**Study #6 — Art Psychotherapy as Adjunctive Depression Treatment (RCT, 2023)**
Randomized controlled trial (N=42) examining art psychotherapy as addition to antidepressant medication in major depressive disorder. Art therapy group showed greater symptom improvement and higher remission rates than medication alone.

**Study #7 — Hobbies and Mental Health (Scoping Review, 2025)**
Scoping review of 11 studies on hobby engagement and mental health. Consistent finding: hobbies reduce depression, anxiety, stress and improve quality of life and social connectedness. Mental health professionals encouraged to promote hobby engagement.

**Study #8 — Crafts-Based Interventions for Mental Health (Systematic Review)**
Systematic review of 19 studies on craft-based mental health interventions. All 19 studies reported short-term improvements in anxiety, stress, depression, self-efficacy, sociability, and mood.

**Study #9 — Digital Interventions for Substance Use Disorder (Meta-Analysis, 2024)**
Scoping review of multiple meta-analyses on digital interventions for substance use. Findings: alcohol consumption reduced 50 standard units/week; smoking cessation improved (RR 1.15-1.80); cannabis use reduced (prevention g = 0.33, treatment g = 0.12); opioid use reduced (g = 0.36); digital CBT for alcohol (g = 0.20-0.30).

**Study #10 — Integrated Digital Interventions for MDD and SUD (Narrative Review, 2025)**
Review of integrated digital treatments for major depressive disorder co-occurring with substance use disorder. Digital integrated treatment combining CBT and motivational interviewing can simultaneously address both conditions. Accessible digital formats meet needs of patients rarely receiving concurrent care.

**Study #11 — Correctional Education and Recidivism (RAND Meta-Analysis, 2013)**
30-year meta-analysis of correctional education research. Inmates in educational programs had 43% lower odds of recidivism (13 percentage-point reduction in reincarceration likelihood). Computer-assisted instruction produced significantly greater gains than traditional instruction.

**Study #12 — Self-Directed Learning in GED Classroom (Auburn University Dissertation, 2024)**
Quantitative research on GED program barriers. GED now fully computerized with mandatory computer-based testing. Computer-based format created barriers for low-income, homeless, and incarcerated populations. Home computer access critical for preparation and testing access.

**Study #13 — Hardware Requirements for Music Production (Technical Analysis, 2024-2025)**
Synthesis of technical specifications for digital audio workstations and music production. Minimum requirements: Intel i5, 16GB RAM, professional audio interface connectivity. Smartphone/tablet cannot substitute—require dedicated laptop for professional audio engineering work.
