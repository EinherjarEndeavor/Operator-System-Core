### **5. Perkins V Career Technical Education Grants & Support**

**Program Name:** Carl D. Perkins Career and Technical Education Act (Perkins V)  
**Funder:** Federal government (U.S. Department of Education)  
**Description:** **Federal funding for Career Technical Education (CTE) programs** explicitly supporting justice-involved learners. States receive allocations; institutions can use funds for tuition, equipment, wraparound services, support for incarcerated/formerly incarcerated students in CTE.

**How It Works (For You):**
- **Direct Benefit:** If you enroll in a CTE program (welding, nursing, HVAC, construction, culinary, etc.) at Oregon community college, your tuition/fees may be partially/fully covered by Perkins V funds
- **No separate application:** Funding flows to institution; you benefit automatically if enrolled in designated CTE program
- **Wraparound services:** Perkins funds also cover tutoring, career coaching, transportation assistance, childcare support

**Eligible CTE Programs (Examples):**
- Welding, electrical work, HVAC, construction trades
- Nursing, medical coding, phlebotomy (healthcare)
- Culinary arts, food service management
- Automotive technology, diesel mechanics
- Information technology, networking, cybersecurity
- Manufacturing, logistics

**Oregon Implementation:**
- Oregon Department of Education oversees Perkins allocation
- Community colleges determine which programs receive Perkins support
- Applied Technology Colleges (ATCs) receive dedicated allocation

**How to Access:**
1. Research CTE programs at target Oregon CC
2. Enroll in Perkins V-funded program (contact financial aid to confirm)
3. Perkins funds automatically reduce your tuition (no separate application required)
4. Ask financial aid office about additional services: tutoring, career coaching, job placement support

**Life-Impact Weight:** 9/10 (if pursuing CTE)  
**Priority Index:** 9.1 (if CTE pathway chosen)  
**[IMMEDIATE IF TRADE CREDENTIAL PATH]**

**Contact:**
- Oregon Department of Education: ode.oregon.gov

## **TIER 6: APPLIED OPPORTUNITIES (No Traditional Scholarships)**

### **7. Paid Apprenticeships & Registered Apprenticeships (Earn + Learn Model)**

**Program Type:** Apprenticeships (Not Scholarships, But Better — You Get Paid While Learning)  
**Funder:** Employers + Federal/State apprenticeship funding  
**Description:** **You earn wages while learning trade.** Registered apprenticeships combine on-the-job training (paid) + classroom instruction (often free/tuition-covered). Common paths: electrician, plumbing, HVAC, carpentry, construction, healthcare. Pay starts $15–$20/hour; increases as you progress through apprenticeship (2–4 years).

**Why Better Than Scholarships:** You're not covering out-of-pocket costs; employer pays you + covers tuition. Net result: income + free education.

**How to Access:**
1. Contact local apprenticeship program (Oregon Apprenticeship & Training Council: oregon.gov/apprenticeship)
2. Or contact union/trade school directly (IBEW for electricians, UA for plumbers, etc.)
3. Apply to apprenticeship program; pass assessment
4. Start earn while you learn

**Example Pathways (Oregon):**
- **Electrician (IBEW Local 48):** $15–$25/hr starting; tuition-free classroom
- **Plumbing (UA Local 290):** $18–$28/hr; tuition covered
- **HVAC:** $16–$22/hr; credential included
- **Construction trades:** $16–$24/hr; pathway to supervisor roles

**Life-Impact Weight:** 9/10 (income + no debt)  
**Priority Index:** 9.4  
**[ALTERNATIVE PATH - Immediate Income + Training]**

**Contact:**
- Oregon Apprenticeship & Training Council: oregon.gov/apprenticeship
- Local trade unions: Search "[trade] apprenticeship Oregon"
- Community colleges: Often administer registered apprenticeships

**Verification:** Federal Department of Labor registered programs | Transparent wage agreements
