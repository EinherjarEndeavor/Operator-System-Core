# How to Sign Up for Portland Community College (PCC)
## A Simplified Guide for Someone in Recovery

---

## PART 1: THE BIG PICTURE

### Why Non-Credit Classes?

If you're new to college and still getting your footing in recovery, **non-credit classes** are your friend. They are:
- Lower stress than degree-seeking credit classes
- Great for exploring interests without heavy academic pressure
- Perfect for artistic, creative, and wellness-focused learning
- Can include things like music, art, dance, photography, woodworking, pottery, creative writing, theater, and mind-body wellness

Once you feel ready, you can transition to credit classes (degree-seeking).

### Non-Credit vs. Credit Classes

**Non-Credit Community Education:**
- Exploration, hobbies, wellness, skill-building
- No prerequisite tests needed
- Cheaper than credit classes
- Usually shorter (4-12 weeks)
- Does NOT count toward a degree, but helps you adjust to college life
- Great first step while in recovery

**Credit Classes:**
- Count toward an Associate degree or certificate
- Lead to job training or transfer to a 4-year school
- Come with more structure and requirements
- Covered by financial aid (grants, loans, scholarships)
- Require placement tests (reading, writing, math)

### Financial Aid: What's Really Available

**You can get financial aid for BOTH non-credit AND credit classes.** Here's what you might qualify for as a 23-year-old in recovery:

| Type | What It Is | Do You Pay It Back? | How Much |
|------|-----------|-------------------|----------|
| **Pell Grant** | Free federal money | NO | Up to $7,395/year (Winter 2026) |
| **Oregon Grant** | Free state money | NO | Varies, based on need |
| **Federal Student Loans** | Borrowed money | YES | Up to $5,500/year (if needed) |
| **PCC Scholarships** | Free money from PCC | NO | $500–$5,000+, varies |
| **Community Scholarships** | Free money from orgs in Oregon | NO | $500–$3,000+, varies |
| **SNAP/STEP Program** | Additional money IF you get SNAP | NO | Up to $100/term for books, supplies, transportation |

### Key Point: Grants and SNAP Money Don't Get Paid Back

The most important thing: **Pell Grants and Oregon Grants are free money.** You do not pay them back. If you qualify, the government pays PCC directly, and any extra goes to you.

## PART 2: THE STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS

### Step 0: Prepare What You'll Need

Before you sit down at a computer to apply, have these ready (or know where to find them):

1. **Your Social Security Number** (if you have one)
2. **Your high school diploma or GED** (the diploma you have is perfect)
3. **Your date of birth**
4. **Your current address** (even if temporary or at a shelter)
5. **An email address** (personal Gmail, Yahoo, etc. – you'll create a PCC email later)
6. **Basic income info** (if you file taxes or receive SNAP, have that info on hand)
7. **Your parent's/guardian's tax info** (if you are a dependent – ask PCC Financial Aid if you're unsure)

**That's it.** Applying to PCC is free and takes 10 minutes.

---

### Step 1: Go to the Library (or Any Computer)

Log in to a public computer. You don't need your own laptop to start.

1. Open a web browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox)
2. Go to: **www.pcc.edu/enroll**
3. You should see a big button that says **"Apply Now"** or similar
4. Click it

**RESULT:** You'll be on the PCC application form.

---

### Step 2: Complete Your Admission Application (10 minutes)

Fill out the form honestly and carefully:

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| Full legal name | Your actual name | Jane Smith |
| Date of birth | MM/DD/YYYY | 03/15/2002 |
| Email address | Your personal email | jane.smith@gmail.com |
| Password | Something you remember | (create a strong one) |
| Phone number | Your number | (555) 555-5555 |
| Current address | Where you're staying now | 123 Main St, Portland, OR 97201 |
| High school | Your high school name | Lincoln High School |
| Graduation year | 2020 (example) | 2020 |
| Do you have a high school diploma? | YES | YES |
| Will you be degree-seeking or non-credit? | BOTH (start with non-credit) | BOTH |

When done, click **Submit** or **Apply**.

**RESULT:** You're officially admitted. Check the email you used—PCC will send you a welcome email.

---

### Step 3: Check Your Email & Set Up MyPCC

1. Go to your personal email (Gmail, Yahoo, etc.)
2. Look for an email from "Portland Community College" or "pcc.edu"
3. Click the link to activate your PCC student account
4. You'll be asked to create a **MyPCC password** (different from your email password—make it something you can remember)
5. Log in to **MyPCC** at **my.pcc.edu** using your PCC email and new password

You now have:
- A PCC student email: **yourname@pcc.edu** (you'll use this for everything at PCC)
- Access to MyPCC (your student portal)

**RESULT:** MyPCC is set up. You can now see your academic record, class schedule, messages, and financial aid status.

---

### Step 4: Complete Orientation (20 minutes)

1. Log in to MyPCC
2. Look for **"Orientation"** or **"Start Here"** link
3. Complete the brief online orientation (usually just videos and quick quizzes)
4. It covers college life, how to use MyPCC, what to expect, and campus resources

**Why do this:** Orientation explains how PCC works, what support is available, and how to navigate campus—especially important for someone in recovery.

**RESULT:** Orientation done. You're cleared to register for classes.

---

### Step 5: Do Placement (Reading, Writing, Math)

PCC uses placement to figure out which level of classes to put you in. This is NOT a test you can fail.

**In MyPCC:**
1. Look for **"Placement"** or **"Assessment"** tab
2. Upload your high school transcript (if you have it) or past test scores
3. You may be asked to take a brief online placement quiz (15 minutes)
4. PCC will recommend what level of reading, writing, and math to start with

**Why:** This keeps you from being thrown into a class that's too hard or too easy. Honest placement = less stress.

**RESULT:** Placement complete. You now know what level of classes to register for.

## PART 3: FINANCIAL AID PROCESS

### The Financial Aid Timeline

- **Step 1:** Fill out FAFSA (January–March = fastest funding)
- **Step 2:** PCC requests extra documents (watch MyPCC)
- **Step 3:** PCC reviews and calculates your award
- **Step 4:** PCC sends you an award letter
- **Step 5:** You accept your award in MyPCC
- **Step 6:** Money goes into your student account (usually before term starts or within 2–3 weeks of term start)

### Step 1: Apply for Financial Aid via FAFSA

**Do this as soon as possible.** The earlier you apply, the more aid you get.

1. Go to **www.studentaid.gov**
2. Click **"FAFSA"**
3. Click **"Start Here"** (or "Create an FSA ID" if first time)
4. Fill out the form. It asks:
   - Your name, SSN, date of birth
   - Your Oregon residency (how long you've lived in Oregon)
   - Your tax information (if you filed taxes; if not, just put $0)
   - Your bank account info (if you have one; if not, leave blank)
   - **Important:** When it asks for school code, enter **011009** (that's Portland Community College's code)

5. Submit and watch for a confirmation email

**Takes about 30 minutes.**

**RESULT:** FAFSA submitted. You'll get a Student Aid Report (SAR) by email.

---

### Step 2: Check Your MyPCC Financial Aid Dashboard

1. Log in to MyPCC
2. Click **"Financial Aid"** tab
3. You'll see three areas:
   - **FAFSA Status:** Says "Received" or "Pending"
   - **Submit Documents:** Might have red flags asking for more info (tax return copies, proof of residency, etc.)
   - **Awards:** Where your grant/loan offer appears once processed

4. **Watch for red flags.** If PCC asks for documents, upload them within 1–2 weeks.

**Common documents PCC might ask for:**
- Proof of Oregon residency (lease, utility bill)
- Copy of last tax return
- School transcript
- Proof of citizenship or visa status (if applicable)

---

### Step 3: Review Your Award Letter

Once PCC processes everything (usually 2–4 weeks after you submit FAFSA), you'll see your **Award Letter** in MyPCC.

It will say something like:

```
Pell Grant: $3,500
Oregon Grant: $1,000
Total: $4,500
```

This is **free money.** PCC will charge it to your tuition bill automatically.

---

### Step 4: Accept Your Award

1. In MyPCC Financial Aid section, click the **Award Letter**
2. Read it carefully
3. Click **"Accept"** or **"I Accept My Award"**

Done. The money is now earmarked for you.

---

### Step 5: Understand How Your Aid Pays Out

**For non-credit classes:** Usually paid out of pocket, but if you're also taking credit classes, your Pell Grant can cover non-credit too.

**For credit classes:** Pell Grant and Oregon Grant automatically pay your tuition. Money left over goes to your student account (you can use it for books, supplies, living expenses, or get a refund).

**Timeline:** Usually the first week of class or within 2–3 weeks.

---

### If You Qualify for SNAP/STEP Program

If you receive SNAP benefits, PCC's **STEP program** gives you extra help:

1. Ask at PCC Counseling or Student Services about STEP
2. You get extra money for:
   - Books and supplies
   - Transportation (bus pass, bike rental)
   - Hygiene products
   - Up to $100/term additional

**This is free money and doesn't affect your SNAP benefits.**

## PART 4: SIGNING UP FOR CLASSES

### Non-Credit Classes (Recommended First)

#### How to Register for Community Education (Non-Credit)

1. Go to **www.pcc.edu/community**
2. Find **"Class Schedule"** or **"Community Education Classes"**
3. Browse categories:
   - Art & Ceramics
   - Music & Theater
   - Wellness & Mind-Body
   - Hobbies & Crafts
   - Creative Writing
   - etc.

4. Click a class that interests you (e.g., "Intro to Photography" or "Pottery Wheel Basics")
5. Click **"Register Now"**
6. Log in with your PCC student email
7. Add to cart and pay (usually $50–$150 per class)

**Important:** You need to register BEFORE the class starts. They do NOT let walk-ins.

---

#### Suggested Non-Credit Classes While Adjusting to College

| Class | Why It's Good | Stress Level |
|-------|---------------|--------------|
| Intro to Photography | Creative outlet, builds skills | Low |
| Pottery or Ceramics | Calming, hands-on, meditative | Low |
| Yoga or Tai Chi | Mind-body wellness, stress relief | Low |
| Creative Writing | Process emotions, build voice | Low-Medium |
| Introduction to Music Production | Tech + creativity, builds confidence | Medium |
| Drawing Fundamentals | Low-tech, meditative, fun | Low |
| Woodworking 101 | Building something physical, grounding | Medium |
| Introduction to Theater | Social connection, expression | Medium |

---

### Credit Classes (After You're Ready)

#### Step 1: Schedule an Advisor Appointment

1. Log in to MyPCC
2. Click **"Advising"** or **"Plan Your Classes"**
3. Book an appointment (in-person or Zoom)
4. Tell your advisor:
   - **"I'm new to college, in recovery, and want a small load to start"** (this is important)
   - **"I'm interested in [art, psychology, computer science, healthcare, etc.]"**
   - **"I want classes that work with my treatment schedule"**

Your advisor will recommend classes based on:
- Your placement scores (reading, writing, math)
- Your interests
- What fits your schedule and recovery commitments

---

#### Step 2: Register for Credit Classes

1. In MyPCC, go to **"Class Schedule"** or **"Register"**
2. Select the term (Winter 2026, Spring 2026, etc.)
3. Add classes to your cart
4. Check that class times don't conflict
5. Review any prerequisites (your advisor will have explained these)
6. Click **"Submit Registration"**

**Tip:** Start with 2–3 classes (6–9 credits). This is NOT a race. You can take 1–2 classes for your first term.

**RESULT:** You're registered. MyPCC shows your schedule. Your financial aid applies automatically.

## PART 5: YOUR STUDENT ID & BENEFITS

### Get Your Digital Student ID

1. Download the **TouchNet 360u app** on your phone (iOS or Android)
2. Open the app and select **Portland Community College**
3. Log in with your PCC email and password
4. Take a front-facing photo (face centered, no hat, no filter, shoulders up)
5. Submit the photo
6. Wait for approval email (usually 24–48 hours)
7. Your digital ID is ready

**What you use your ID for:**
- Library checkouts
- Tutoring centers
- Campus shuttle
- Student discounts
- Campus food pantries

## PART 6: SUPPORT FOR RECOVERY

### Counseling at PCC

1. Call **PCC Counseling: (971) 722-7060**
2. Or go in-person to the Counseling Center
3. Tell them: **"I'm in recovery and want support"**

**Available services:**
- Drop-in counseling (no appointment needed)
- Individual counseling
- Group counseling (including recovery groups)
- Stress management workshops
- Mental health support
- Substance abuse recovery support

**Cost:** FREE for enrolled students. Confidential.

---

### PCC's Addiction Resources Page

Go to **www.pcc.edu/counseling/addiction-resources**

This lists:
- Local treatment programs
- Support groups (AA, NA, SMART Recovery)
- Crisis hotlines
- Recovery coaches
- Mental health resources

---

### Disability Services & Accessibility

If you have accommodation needs (ADHD, anxiety, PTSD, hearing loss, etc.):
1. Contact **Accessible Education & Disability Resources**
2. Request accommodations (deadline extensions, quiet testing space, note-taker, etc.)
3. All accommodations are confidential

## PART 9: FINAL REMINDERS

### You Are Not Alone

- PCC has counseling, support groups, and recovery programs specifically for students like you
- **Call counseling TODAY** if you need support—don't wait
- The Basic Needs Center has food, housing, and transportation help
- Your student ID unlocks free resources you've already paid for (via activity fee)

### Start Small

- One non-credit class while settling in = perfectly valid
- A small credit load (2–3 classes) is better than overloading
- Recovery comes first; school is secondary

### Grants Don't Get Paid Back

- Pell Grant + Oregon Grant = free money
- This is not about debt; this is money the government gives YOU
- Loans (if you take them) do need repayment—ask your advisor before borrowing

### You Have Support

- PCC Counseling is free and confidential
- Your advisor wants you to succeed
- Other students are in recovery too; you're not alone

---

**Good luck. You've got this.**
