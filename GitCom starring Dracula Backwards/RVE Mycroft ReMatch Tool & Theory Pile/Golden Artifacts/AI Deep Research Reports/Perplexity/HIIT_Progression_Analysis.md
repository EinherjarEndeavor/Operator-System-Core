## PART 2: METHODOLOGY COMPARISON

### TABATA (20 seconds work, 10 seconds rest)

**Total Session Duration Options:**
- **4 minutes:** 8 rounds × 1 exercise = minimal BDNF
- **8 minutes:** 2 sets of 4 minutes × different exercises
- **12 minutes:** 3 sets = moderate BDNF elevation
- **16 minutes:** 4 sets = optimal BDNF

**Immediate Effects (Tabata 8 min):**
- **Energy:** +70% (ATP-PC dominant, minimal glycolytic fatigue)
- **Focus:** +75% (sharp catecholamine spike, short duration)
- **Dopamine:** +65% (moderate intensity adequate)
- **BDNF:** +20-25% (Tabata's micro-intervals = efficient BDNF stimulus)
- **Lactate:** Moderate-High (75-80 mmol/L, rapid buffer recovery)
- **Recovery:** FAST (active recovery 20 sec adequate, minimal soreness)

**Long-term (12 weeks, 3x/week):**
- **VO2max gain:** +12-18% (Tabata especially effective)
- **Mitochondrial adaptation:** +40-50%
- **Overtraining risk:** LOW (efficient work-rest ratio)

**Best For:** Time-efficient maximum intensity, strict interval work, controlled recovery

---

### EMOM (Every Minute On The Minute)

**Total Session Duration Options:**
- **10 minutes:** 10 exercises, 1 rep each per minute (light)
- **15 minutes:** Mix of 2-4 movements, scaled reps
- **20 minutes:** Complex scaling (more reps as you fatigue = self-limiting)
- **30 minutes:** Advanced, high overtraining risk

**Immediate Effects (EMOM 15 min, 3 exercises):**
- **Energy:** +72% (varied intensity, built-in recovery windows)
- **Focus:** +78% (pacing demand high, mental engagement)
- **Dopamine:** +68% (moderate-high, consistent)
- **BDNF:** +22-28% (consistent intensity, less spike than Tabata)
- **Lactate:** Moderate (65-75 mmol/L, can recover during rest windows)
- **Recovery:** MODERATE (pacing flexibility, lower soreness than Tabata)

**Long-term (12 weeks, 2-3x/week):**
- **VO2max gain:** +10-15%
- **Mitochondrial adaptation:** +35-45%
- **Work capacity:** +20-30% (sustained output under fatigue)
- **Overtraining risk:** LOW-MODERATE (depends on scaling difficulty)

**Best For:** Pacing practice, varied movements, controlled intensity scaling

---

### AMRAP (As Many Reps As Possible)

**Total Session Duration Options:**
- **5 minutes:** Sprint-focused (anaerobic/glycolytic)
- **10 minutes:** Mixed energy systems
- **15 minutes:** Extended aerobic demand
- **20 minutes:** Very high overtraining risk

**Immediate Effects (AMRAP 10 min, 4 movements):**
- **Energy:** +75% (competitive drive boosts perceived energy)
- **Focus:** +80% (time pressure + rep counting = high mental load)
- **Dopamine:** +72% (competitive stimulus elevated)
- **BDNF:** +20-26% (variable intensity, less controlled)
- **Lactate:** High (80-85 mmol/L, pacing unknown = unpredictable)
- **Recovery:** HIGH soreness risk (pacing often poor, excessive lactate)

**Long-term (12 weeks, 2x/week):**
- **VO2max gain:** +8-12%
- **Mitochondrial adaptation:** +30-40%
- **Mental toughness:** +20-30% (grit under fatigue)
- **Overtraining risk:** MODERATE-HIGH (unpredictable pacing risk)

**Best For:** Mental resilience, competitive drive, variable stimulus
