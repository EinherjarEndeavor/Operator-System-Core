## STAIRMASTER vs. INCLINE TREADMILL: RESEARCH DECISION

**Your Question**: "Can stairmaster warmup → stairmaster HIIT finish work? Or should I use incline treadmill?"

**Research Comparison**:

| Factor | Stairmaster | Incline Treadmill |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Muscle Recruitment** | **More (glutes, quads, hamstrings, core)** | Moderate (quads, glutes primarily) |
| **Joint Impact** | Low (weight-bearing but controlled) | Low-moderate (depends on speed) |
| **Calorie Burn** | High (272 cal/30 min for 150 lb person) | High (170-300 cal/30 min depending on incline) |
| **BDNF Stimulus** | **Higher** (more muscle engagement = more irisin release)[web:417][web:425] | Moderate-high |
| **Dopamine Pathway Activation** | **Direct + intense** (higher effort perception)[web:422] | Direct but less intense |
| **Lower Body Strength Gain** | **Superior** (vertical resistance)[web:427] | Good (with high incline) |
| **Combat Applicability** | **Better** (lower body power for kicks/footwork) | Good (leg endurance) |
| **Core Engagement** | **Requires active stability** (without holding handles) | Minimal core engagement |
| **Accessibility** | Gym equipment (most gyms have) | Gym equipment (all gyms have) |
| **Joint Stress** | Very low-impact | Low-impact |

**Verdict**: **Stairmaster > Incline Treadmill for your goals**[web:422][web:424][web:427][web:430]

**Why**:
1. Higher muscle recruitment = more BDNF spike[web:425]
2. More effort required = greater dopamine response[web:422]
3. Better leg power development (combat-relevant)
4. Lower joint stress
5. Core engagement (no hand-holding = stability demand)

**Exception**: Use incline treadmill IF joints hurt on stairmaster, or if want variety (alternate weekly)

## STAIRMASTER HIIT PROTOCOL: DETAILED

### Pre-Stairmaster (Transition Phase)

**After completing DoD strength work:**
1. Walk for 2-3 minutes (HR drops slightly, ~110-120 bpm from ~150-160)
2. Drink water + electrolyte (maintains hydration, prevents drop in performance)
3. Light dynamic stretching (hip circles, leg swings)
4. Step on stairmaster, light pace for 1 minute to acclimate

**Goal**: Heart rate ~120-130 before starting intervals (fresh but primed)

---

### Stairmaster HIIT Warm-up (3 minutes)

| Minute | Intensity | RPM | Effort |
|--------|-----------|-----|--------|
| **1** | Light | 50-60 | 3/10 |
| **2** | Moderate | 60-70 | 5/10 |
| **3** | Brisk | 70-80 | 6/10 |

**Goal**: Elevate HR to 130-140 bpm, prepare for intervals

---

### Main HIIT Work (12-15 minutes)

**Best Protocol for Addiction Recovery + Cognition**: 30 sec work : 60 sec recovery (intense but sustainable)[web:422][web:425]

**Structure**: 30 sec MAX EFFORT → 60 sec EASY RECOVERY × 10-12 intervals

#### Interval Breakdown:

| Interval | Phase | Duration | Intensity | RPM Target | HR Target | Notes |
|----------|-------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **1 Sprint** | Work | 30 sec | 9-10/10 | 100-110 | 170-180 | Max effort, full speed |
| **1 Recovery** | Recovery | 60 sec | 2-3/10 | 50-60 | 130-140 | Light, catch breath |
| **2 Sprint** | Work | 30 sec | 9-10/10 | 100-110 | 170-180 | Maintain speed |
| **2 Recovery** | Recovery | 60 sec | 2-3/10 | 50-60 | 130-140 | Active recovery |
| **3-10 Sprints** | Repeat | 30 sec × 8 | 9-10/10 | 100-110 | 170-180 | Same pattern |
| **10 Recoveries** | Repeat | 60 sec × 8 | 2-3/10 | 50-60 | 130-140 | Same pattern |

**Total Work**: 10-12 × (30 sec work + 60 sec recovery) = 12-15 minutes

**Total Stairmaster Time**: 3 min warm-up + 12-15 min HIIT = 15-18 minutes

---

## WARM-UP CONSIDERATION: "Should I warm up on stairmaster or use a separate warmup?"

**Research**: Pre-HIIT warm-up intensity matters. **Light warm-up required but NOT heavy**.

**Your Setup**: 
- DoD strength WORK already elevates HR/CNS
- Transition walk (2-3 min) brings HR down slightly (allows recovery before HIIT)
- Stairmaster 3-min warm-up = sufficient (don't over-warm, save energy for main work)

**Why Not "Stairmaster Warmup → Stairmaster HIIT"**:
- Back-to-back stairmaster = lower body fatigued before HIIT intervals
- Defeats purpose: HIIT needs fresh fast-twitch fibers for max dopamine/BDNF response
- Your DoD strength already warmed everything up

**BETTER**: DoD upper body → light transition → fresh stairmaster with built-in 3-min warmup
