## PART 1: DETAILED ACTION PLAN (COMPLETE GUIDANCE)

### ⭐ PRIORITY TIER 1: IMMEDIATE (DO WITHIN 24-48 HOURS)

#### ACTION 1: Contact Social Security for SSI Verification/Application
**Life Impact:** 10/10 (Foundational—everything depends on this)
**Effort Required:** Low
**Time to Complete:** 30 days (application); verify within 24 hours if already approved
**ACT NOW Score:** 1 (MOST CRITICAL)

**Why This Matters:**
SSI is your income floor ($967/month guaranteed) that enables ALL other programs. It automatically triggers Medicaid eligibility, housing assistance qualification, and work incentives. Without it, you're trapped in poverty cycle.

**What to Do:**
1. Call: **1-800-772-1213** (Social Security main line) or **503-640-7000** (Portland local office)
2. Say: "I was recently released from incarceration and need to verify my Supplemental Security Income (SSI) status or apply if I haven't already."
3. Have ready:
   - Full legal name & date of birth
   - Social Security number
   - Prison release documentation (letter from facility)
   - Proof of address (even temporary housing acceptable)
4. Ask specifically: "Am I eligible for Interim Assistance Reimbursement (IAR) while my SSI is pending?" (County pays housing during waiting period; automatically repaid from back-pay)

**How to Say It (Script):**
> "Hi, my name is [name]. I was released from [facility name] on [date] and I believe I have SSI eligibility. Can you verify my current status or help me apply immediately? I'm now living in Washington County, Oregon, and I'm on probation. I need to understand what benefits I qualify for right now, and whether the county can help with housing costs while my application processes."

**When:**
Call TODAY. Mornings (8am-11am PST) have shorter wait times.

**Where:**
- By phone: 1-800-772-1213
- Or visit: Federal Building, 511 NW Broadway, Portland OR 97209 (appointment required)
- Online: ssa.gov (create account to check status)

**Email/Follow-up:**
After initial call, request written confirmation of:
- Application receipt (you should get receipt number)
- Estimated decision date
- Your case worker's name & direct line
- IAR eligibility and application process

**What You Get:**
- ✅ $967/month guaranteed income (2025)
- ✅ Automatic Oregon Health Plan (OHP) Plus enrollment
- ✅ Medicaid covers all healthcare at no cost
- ✅ Automatic qualification for housing assistance programs
- ✅ Work Incentives (first $65 earned income doesn't reduce SSI)

**Unique Advantage:**
SSI cannot be reduced or taken away based on other income (unlike SNAP which has income limits). This is your permanent safety net.

### ⭐ PRIORITY TIER 2: URGENT (DO WITHIN 1-2 WEEKS)

#### ACTION 4: Secure Housing (MOST COMPLEX—Start Immediately)
**Life Impact:** 10/10 (Cannot function without housing)
**Effort Required:** HIGH
**Time to Complete:** 30-45 days
**ACT NOW Score:** 2 (START SAME TIME AS TIER 1)

**Why This Matters:**
Housing is the ONE variable that determines everything. Unstable housing = probation violations, missed healthcare, lost employment. Stable housing = compliance, health, dignity, employment opportunity.

**Best Options in Priority Order:**

**OPTION A: IRISS Housing (If Qualified)**
- Supervised reentry housing + case management
- Cost: $0-$300/month (sliding scale)
- Contact: **Washington County Community Corrections (503-846-4300)**
  - Ask: "Does IRISS include housing placement?"
  - Typical: 6-12 months transitional, then help moving to independent housing
- Timeline: 14-30 days if approved
- Unique: Intensive supervision + recovery mentors on-site

**OPTION B: Oxford House (Drug-Free Peer Housing)**
- Democratically-run, self-supporting (not facility-based—no SSI suspension!)
- Cost: $400-600/month (covered by SSI + SNAP)
- Process:
  1. Find houses: oxfordhouse.org (search Oregon > Washington County)
  2. Call house directly, ask about vacancies
  3. Attend house meeting (interview with residents)
  4. Residents vote on admission
  5. Move in if approved
- Timeline: 7-14 days from first call
- Contact: Oxford House International (1-800-689-6411)
- Unique: Peer support + zero substance use enforcement

**OPTION C: Rent Guarantee Fund (If Renting Privately)**
- County pays first/last month + security deposit + 12 months rent insurance
- Income requirement: 60% AMI or less ($1,200/month for single in Washington County)
- Requirements:
  1. Complete RentWell course (8 weeks, FREE)
  2. Be homeless or unstably housed
  3. Have barriers (criminal history, poor credit, eviction—CHECK, CHECK, CHECK)
- Contact: **rentwell.org**
  - Phone: 1-844-797-3638
  - Start course IMMEDIATELY
- Process: Enroll in RentWell (1-2 days) → Complete course (8 weeks) → Apply for fund → Find rental → Move in
- Timeline: 10-12 weeks total
- Cost to you: $0 for course; $300-400/month rent is PAID BY COUNTY for first year
- Unique: Path to PERMANENT independent housing

**OPTION D: Project Homeless Connect Washington County**
- Immediate shelter + case management
- THREE one-day resource events annually (Hillsboro, Beaverton, Tigard)
- Contact: **phcwc.org**
- Next event: Check website for dates
- Process: Attend event → Meet case manager → Get connected to programs
- Cost: FREE
- Unique: One-stop shop (dental, haircuts, housing, legal aid, all in 1 day)

**OPTION E: Emergency Winter Shelter (If It's Severe Weather)**
- December-March open on dangerous weather nights
- Contact: **Project Homeless Connect (dial 211)** or **WC Crisis Line (503-291-9111)**
- Cost: FREE
- Unique: Immediate safety when needed most

**What to Do RIGHT NOW:**
1. **If PO recommends IRISS housing:** Follow PO's referral (fastest path)
2. **If not in IRISS:** Start RentWell course TODAY
   - Call 1-844-797-3638
   - Say: "I'm a recently incarcerated senior on SSI in Washington County. I want to enroll in RentWell immediately to prepare for independent housing."
3. **WHILE waiting for RentWell/housing:**
   - Call Oxford Houses directly (5-10 calls): oxfordhouse.org

**Documents You'll Need:**

**When:**
START calling Oxford Houses IMMEDIATELY (within 24 hours of release, if possible).
START RentWell enrollment within 1 week (if not in IRISS).

**Where:**
Oxford House (national): oxfordhouse.org or 1-800-689-6411
RentWell: rentwell.org or 1-844-797-3638

**What You Get:**

#### ACTION 7: Register with Oregon Work Incentives Network (WIN)
**Life Impact:** 7/10 (Employment without penalty)
**Effort Required:** Low
**Time to Complete:** 7 days
**ACT NOW Score:** 3 (WITHIN 3 WEEKS)

**Why This Matters:**
WIN is FREE counseling that teaches you how to work while keeping your SSI. Without WIN, you might lose benefits thinking you can't work. With WIN, you can earn $500-1000/month and STILL get SSI ($759-967 depending on earnings).

**What to Do:**
1. Contact: **Work Incentives Network**
   - Online: **ilr.org/win/**
   - Or call: **1-866-543-WIN (9466)**
   - Or visit: Oregon ODHS office (201 N.E. Third Ave, Hillsboro)
2. Say: "I'm an SSI recipient who was recently released from incarceration and I plan to work. I need help understanding work incentives so I don't lose my benefits."
3. Request:
   - Information & Referral (I&R) consultation (FREE, 30 minutes)
   - OR Comprehensive Benefits analysis (MORE detailed, still FREE)
   - OR Work Incentive Plan (WIP) if planning specific job

**When:**
Register AFTER housing is secure (hard to use work incentives if homeless).
Register BEFORE starting job (so you report correctly to SSA).

**Where:**
- Online: ilr.org/win/ (self-service option)
- By phone: 1-866-543-9466
- In person: Contact local ODHS office or community partner

**What You Get:**
- ✅ Understanding of: SSI work incentive rules
- ✅ How much you can earn without losing SSI
- ✅ Which WAGES don't count ($20 + $65 per month excluded)
- ✅ How to report earnings to SSA correctly
- ✅ Plan to work while maintaining health insurance

**Example (WIN Scenario):**
- SSI base: $967
- You earn: $500/month
- Amount excluded: $20 + $65 = $85
- Countable earnings: $500 - $85 = $415
- SSI reduction: $415 × 50% = $207.50
- **Your actual monthly income: $759.50 (SSI) + $500 (earnings) = $1,259.50**
- (vs. $967 if not working)

**Unique Advantage:**
This is FREE financial counseling that helps you EARN MORE while keeping benefits. Most people don't know about it.

**Why This Matters:**

#### ACTION 14: File for Expungement (If Eligible - CRIMINAL RECORD REMOVAL)
**Life Impact:** 7/10 (Employment/housing barrier removal)
**Effort Required:** Medium
**Time to Complete:** 60-120 days
**ACT NOW Score:** 5 (DO AFTER STABILITY, BEFORE JOB SEARCH)

**Why This Matters:**
Expungement SEALS your criminal record. Employers and landlords can't see it. This opens doors for jobs and housing that would otherwise reject you.

**Who's Eligible:**
- Arrests with NO conviction (instant after arrest dismissed)
- Convictions (varies by crime):
  - Most crimes: eligible 3 years after sentence completion
  - Violent felonies: may not be eligible
  - Sex offenses: may not be eligible
  - Ask probation officer: "Am I eligible for expungement?"

**What to Do:**
1. Call: **Washington County District Attorney (503-846-3900)**
   - Ask: "Do I qualify for expungement? What's the process?"
2. Or: **Oregon Law Help (oregonlawhelp.org)**
   - Link to free expungement clinics (they do filings FOR FREE)
3. Or: **pdxlrg.org/expungements** (Portland Law Resources Group)
   - Free legal clinics (regular schedule)
4. Process:
   - Pay $50-100 court filing fee (waivers available)
   - Probation officer usually doesn't oppose (benefit to probation)
   - Judge approves (usually automatic if eligible)
   - Records DESTROYED (not visible on background checks)

**How to Say It (To Court):**
> "I have been out of the criminal justice system for [months/years]. I have been compliant with probation. Expungement will help me secure employment and housing, which will help me stay out of the system. I respectfully request the court to set aside this conviction."

**Unique Advantage:**
After expungement, you can legally say "I have no criminal record" on job applications. This dramatically increases hire rates.

#### ACTION 15: File Oregon Tax Returns (CLAIM EITC & RETIREMENT INCOME CREDIT)
**Life Impact:** 6/10 ($2,000-3,600 annual refund)
**Effort Required:** Medium
**Time to Complete:** 120 days (January-April tax season)
**ACT NOW Score:** 5 (DO NEXT TAX SEASON)

**Why This Matters:**
If you work any amount, you can get $2,000-3,600 back from state/federal taxes. That's a BONUS on top of SSI. This does NOT reduce SSI, SNAP, or Medicaid.

**What to Do:**
1. If you work ANY AMOUNT:
   - Save all pay stubs
   - Note earnings from 2025
2. File 2025 tax return in 2026 (April 15):
   - Online: TurboTax, TaxAct (FREE versions available)
   - In person: Local tax preparation clinic (nonprofits do FREE tax prep)
   - Contact: 211 for "free tax preparation" in Washington County
3. Claim:
   - Federal Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC): $2,210-3,656
   - Oregon Earned Income Credit (EIC): 12% of federal EITC (~$265-439)
   - Retirement Income Credit: If 62+ and receiving retirement income

**Example:**
- You earn: $10,000 in 2025
- Federal EITC: $3,656
- Oregon EIC: $439
- Total refund: $4,095
- **This is FREE MONEY the government gives you back**

**Important:**
⚠️ **This DOES NOT count as income for:**
- SSI calculation
- Medicaid
- SNAP
- Public housing

**When:**
File January 1 - April 15 (tax season).
But START planning October (get pay stubs organized).

**Where:**
- Online: IRS.gov or state tax prep sites
- By phone: Oregon Department of Revenue (1-800-356-4222)
- Free in-person: Search "free tax prep Washington County" or call 211

**What You Get:**
- ✅ $2,000-3,600 annual refund
- ✅ Does not affect SSI/SNAP/Medicaid
- ✅ Counts as BONUS income (can save or spend freely)

**Unique Advantage:**
This is FREE money. You earned it through work. Most people don't know about it because they don't file taxes.

## WHEN THINGS GO WRONG

**Benefits Denied:**
→ Call Washington County DAVS (503-846-3060) to appeal
→ They'll represent you at hearing

**Probation Violation Threatened:**
→ Call Washington County Crisis Line IMMEDIATELY (503-291-9111)
→ Or tell your probation officer BEFORE a violation occurs

**Eviction/Housing Crisis:**
→ Call Community Action (503-615-0771)
→ Or Project Homeless Connect (phcwc.org)
→ Or WC Housing (washingtoncountyor.gov/housing)

**Health Crisis:**
→ Call 911 or go to nearest ER
→ OHP covers all emergency care

**Mental Health Crisis:**
→ Call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
→ Or 503-291-9111 (WC Crisis Line)

**Substance Use Crisis:**
→ Call SAMHSA: 1-800-662-4357
→ Or local treatment center directly
