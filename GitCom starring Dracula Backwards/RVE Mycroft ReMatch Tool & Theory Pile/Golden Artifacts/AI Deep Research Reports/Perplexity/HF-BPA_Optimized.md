# HF-BPA OPTIMIZED: TEMPO-POWER-HIP PROTOCOL
## Weeks 1-12 Foundation Phase (Combat-Ready Strength & Power Development)

---

## OVERVIEW

**Core Principle:** Tempo-based eccentric loading (force) + explosive concentrics (velocity) + dedicated hip/calf development + minimal ballistic maintenance work.

**AM Session:** Standalone foundational work. Can function independently.  
**PM Session:** Bolstering/capacity-building. Optional for advanced recovery; mandatory for max adaptations.

**Key Changes from HF-BPA:**
- Tempo control (3-2-1-0 & 2-0-X) for force-velocity curve optimization
- Hip extension + hip flexion + calf isolation 2-3x/week
- Ballistic work (2x/week, ultra-low volume) prevents power detraining
- Metabolic stress variance every 2 weeks
- Combat skill work on Day 4 (fresh CNS)

---

## PROGRAM OPTIONS

### **OPTION A: BOTH SESSIONS MANDATORY**
- Best for maximum adaptation, 6-12 week block
- Total time: ~2 hours/day
- Requires above-average recovery (sleep, nutrition, hydration)
- Recommended for dedicated foundation phase

### **OPTION B: AM STANDALONE, PM OPTIONAL**
- AM = complete, balanced stimulus
- PM = added capacity/volume for those who can handle it
- Flexibility: skip PM on fatigue days without losing progress
- Total time: 60-75 min (AM only) to 2 hours (both)
- Recommended for those managing recovery constraints

### **OPTION C: ALTERNATING STRUCTURE (3 Days Full, 3 Days AM-Only)**
- Days 1, 4, 7: AM only
- Days 2, 3, 5, 6: AM + PM
- Reduces cumulative fatigue, maintains adaptations
- Total weekly time: ~9-10 hours
- Recommended for balanced progress without overtraining

## PROGRESSION STRATEGY (Weeks 2-12)

### Weeks 1-2: Tempo Mastery & Movement Quality
- Focus: Perfect tempo execution, form development
- Load: 60-70% of max
- Auto-regulation: If form breaks, reduce load

### Weeks 3-4: Intensity Increase (Weak-Point Heavy)
- Weak-point loads: 75-85% intensity
- Isolation loads: +5-10% from Week 1
- Ballistic reps: Same (power maintenance)

### Weeks 5-6: Metabolic Stress Variance (Tempo Swap)
- Change isolation tempo every 2 weeks to prevent accommodation
- Week 5-6 isolation option: 4:0:2:0 (very slow eccentric, fast concentric) instead of 3:1:2:0
- Maintains same reps, different stimulus

### Weeks 7-8: Weak-Point Rotation
- Rotate weak-point exercises monthly (prevent accommodation)
- Day 2 weak-point: Skull Crushers → Overhead Press Negatives
- Day 3 weak-point: Face Pulls → Chest Flyes
- Day 5 weak-point: Hip Thrusts → Bulgarian Split Squats
- Day 6 weak-point: Reverse Hypers → Leg Press

### Weeks 9-10: Ballistic Volume Increase
- Power reps: 3 → 4-5 per set (still 3-4 sets, but more reps)
- Speed target: Maintain 0.8+ m/s movement velocity
- Load: Same (ballistic work focuses on speed, not load)

### Weeks 11-12: Deload + Test Week
- Week 11: Reduce all loads by 20%, focus on recovery
- Week 12: Test new maxes on weak-point heavy work and ballistic exercises

## NUTRITION & RECOVERY SUPPORT

### Daily Requirements
- **Protein:** 0.8-1.0g per lb bodyweight (divided across meals)
- **Carbs:** 2-3g per lb bodyweight (fuel for 2-hour training sessions)
- **Fat:** 0.3-0.4g per lb bodyweight (hormone regulation)
- **Hydration:** Half bodyweight in oz water daily + 500ml per hour training

### Supplement Stack (Optional but Recommended)
- **Creatine:** 5g daily (strength + power)
- **Beetroot/Pomegranate:** NO (not needed; good sleep + water sufficient)
- **Magnesium Glycinate:** 300-400mg before bed (sleep quality + recovery)
- **Melatonin:** 2-3mg, 30 min before bed (offset nicotine circadian disruption)

### Sleep Optimization
- **Target:** 7-9 hours nightly
- **Nicotine timing:** No use after 2 PM if possible (preserve night sleep)
- **Pre-bed routine:** Lights off 30 min before bed, cool room (65-68°F)
- **HRV tracking:** Use Whoop/Oura if available; if resting HR > baseline + 10 bpm, reduce next day's PM session to AM-only

### Recovery Signals (Autoregulation)
- **Continue training:** Resting HR normal, sleep quality good, mood stable
- **Reduce PM session intensity:** Resting HR +5-7 bpm, sleep fragmented, slight mood dip
- **Downgrade to AM only (skip PM):** Resting HR +10+ bpm, sleep severely disrupted, fatigue evident
- **Take full rest day:** Persistent elevated HR, injury pain (not muscle soreness), acute illness

## TRACKING & FORM CUES

### Tempo Execution
- Use phone timer or audible metronome (apps: "Tempo" by iOS, "BPM Counter")
- Count aloud during first 3 reps of each set (builds habit)
- Video record 1 set per exercise per week to verify form

### Load Tracking
- Write down weights used for each exercise (simple notebook or phone notes)
- Goal: Maintain loads Week 1 (form focus), increase 5-10% Weeks 3-4, maintain Weeks 5-8, re-test Weeks 11-12

### Injury Prevention
- **RED FLAGS:** Sharp pain (not muscle burn), joint discomfort, swelling
- **Response:** Skip that exercise, use substitution, assess after 2-3 days
- **Preventive:** Warm-up 5 min stairmaster before any session, 10 min mobility post-training

## FINAL NOTES

1. **AM is standalone:** If life happens and PM is impossible, AM session = complete training day. You'll still progress, just slower.

2. **Tempo is non-negotiable:** This is where the force-velocity curve optimization happens. Fast eccentric + slow concentric is different from what you see in typical programs. Commit to it.

3. **Weak-point work is your edge:** Most people skip heavy accessory work. This is your competitive advantage for speed/force gains.

4. **Power maintenance is minimum effective dose:** 2-3x/week ballistic work prevents the decay that happens when you defer all explosive work. Recovery better than re-learning.
